EXCERPT 1: LIQUIDITY RISK AND CAPITAL ADEQUACY
================================================================================

Item 7A. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk — Liquidity

Meridian Financial Corp maintains a liquidity coverage ratio (LCR) of 134% as of December 31, 2023, exceeding the regulatory minimum of 100% mandated under Basel III. The Company holds $2.84 billion in high-quality liquid assets (HQLA), comprising $1.62 billion in U.S. Treasury securities and $1.22 billion in agency mortgage-backed securities. Net cash outflows over a 30-day stress scenario are estimated at $2.12 billion, based on assumptions derived from the Company's historical deposit runoff rates during periods of elevated market volatility.

Deposits from retail customers constitute 61.3% of total funding as of year-end, totaling $18.7 billion. Wholesale funding reliance is limited to $4.2 billion, or 13.8% of total liabilities, a decrease from 16.4% in fiscal year 2022. The Company maintains committed credit facilities totaling $850 million from a consortium of eight correspondent banks, none of which were drawn upon during fiscal year 2023.

Management believes that existing liquidity resources, combined with anticipated cash flows from operations, are sufficient to meet the Company's obligations for at least the next twelve months. However, an unanticipated acceleration in deposit withdrawals exceeding $3.5 billion within a 90-day window would require the Company to activate contingency funding plans, which include monetization of the available-for-sale (AFS) investment portfolio and borrowings under the Federal Home Loan Bank (FHLB) facility currently rated at a maximum drawable capacity of $6.1 billion.

Interest rate risk is monitored through a Net Interest Income (NII) sensitivity model. A parallel shift of +200 basis points in the yield curve is projected to reduce annual NII by $47.3 million, representing a 3.2% compression from the base case of $1.48 billion. A downward shift of -100 basis points would reduce NII by $29.8 million due to the Company's asset-sensitive balance sheet positioning.

EXCERPT 2: REVENUE BREAKDOWN AND SEGMENT PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

Item 8. Financial Statements and Supplementary Data — Revenue Disaggregation

For fiscal year 2023, Meridian Financial Corp recorded total net revenue of $3.21 billion, representing a 7.4% increase from $2.99 billion in fiscal year 2022. Revenue is disaggregated across four principal business segments as follows:

Commercial Banking contributed $1.42 billion (44.2% of total revenue), driven by loan originations of $8.3 billion, of which commercial real estate loans constituted $3.6 billion (43.4% of originations) and commercial and industrial (C&I) loans constituted $2.9 billion (34.9%). Net interest margin for the Commercial Banking segment expanded 18 basis points to 3.47% compared to 3.29% in the prior year, reflecting higher short-term rate pass-through on floating-rate C&I credits.

Retail Banking contributed $986 million (30.7% of total revenue). Mortgage originations totaled $4.1 billion in fiscal 2023, a decline of 22.3% from $5.3 billion in fiscal 2022, consistent with the industrywide contraction in refinancing activity. Non-interest income from service charges and card interchange fees reached $213 million, up 8.7% year-over-year.

Wealth Management and Trust Services contributed $487 million (15.2% of total revenue), benefiting from market appreciation and $1.2 billion in net new assets under management (AUM). Total AUM reached $31.4 billion at year-end, a 14.6% increase from $27.4 billion at December 31, 2022.

Capital Markets and Treasury contributed $315 million (9.8% of total revenue), with gains on sale from the AFS portfolio of $42 million partially offset by $28 million in unrealized mark-to-market losses on interest rate swaps used in the loan hedging program.

EXCERPT 3: OPERATIONAL RISKS AND CYBERSECURITY
================================================================================

Item 1A. Risk Factors — Operational and Technology Risk

The Company is exposed to operational risk arising from inadequate or failed internal processes, systems, human errors, or external events. During fiscal year 2023, the Company recorded operational loss events totaling $14.7 million, net of insurance recoveries, compared to $11.2 million in fiscal year 2022. The increase is primarily attributable to a $6.3 million settlement related to a legacy mortgage servicing process error identified during a regulatory examination conducted in the second quarter of 2023.

Cybersecurity represents a material risk to the Company's operations. The Company invested $78.3 million in information security infrastructure during fiscal year 2023, a 23.4% increase from the prior year, reflecting enhanced endpoint detection and response (EDR) capabilities and expanded zero-trust network architecture deployment across 1,247 branch and back-office locations. The Company employs 186 dedicated cybersecurity professionals and maintains a 24/7 Security Operations Center (SOC) with a mean time to detect (MTTD) of 4.2 minutes for critical severity events.

In October 2023, the Company detected and successfully contained a phishing attack targeting 34 branch employees. No customer data was compromised, and system integrity was maintained. The incident required 612 employee-hours of remediation and $340,000 in external forensic consulting fees. The Company has reported this incident to the appropriate regulatory bodies in accordance with OCC guidance effective November 2023.

Third-party vendor risk is actively managed through a vendor risk program encompassing 847 vendors, of which 62 are designated as critical vendors subject to enhanced due diligence. Annual on-site assessments were completed for all 62 critical vendors during fiscal year 2023. No material control deficiencies were identified at critical vendors during the assessment cycle.

EXCERPT 4: MANAGEMENT OUTLOOK AND STRATEGIC PRIORITIES
================================================================================

Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis — Forward-Looking Statements and Strategic Outlook

Management projects total revenue growth in the range of 4% to 7% for fiscal year 2024, predicated on a base case assumption of two Federal Reserve rate cuts of 25 basis points each in the second half of 2024 and continued loan growth in the Commercial Banking segment of 6% to 8%. These projections are subject to material uncertainty and should not be construed as guarantees of future performance.

The Company's strategic priorities for 2024 are organized around three pillars. First, accelerating digital channel adoption: the Company's mobile banking application recorded 2.3 million monthly active users in December 2023, a 31% increase from December 2022. Management targets 3.0 million monthly active users by December 2024 and expects digital channel transactions to represent 74% of total retail transaction volume by year-end, compared to 67% as of December 31, 2023.

Second, expanding the Wealth Management segment: the Company has engaged two Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) firms for potential acquisition, with combined AUM of approximately $4.8 billion. Completion of at least one acquisition is targeted for the second half of 2024 at an estimated purchase price of $210 million to $260 million, representing a 4.4x to 5.4x AUM multiple, consistent with prevailing market valuations for similar transactions.

Third, credit quality management: the allowance for credit losses (ACL) stands at $487 million, or 1.24% of total loans, as of December 31, 2023. The Company's net charge-off (NCO) rate of 0.31% for fiscal year 2023 compares favorably to the peer median of 0.44% for commercial banks with total assets between $25 billion and $100 billion. Management anticipates the NCO rate will increase modestly to 0.38% to 0.45% in fiscal year 2024 given normalization of consumer credit quality following the pandemic-era support period.

EXCERPT 5: DEBT OBLIGATIONS AND CONTRACTUAL COMMITMENTS
================================================================================

Item 8. Financial Statements — Note 12: Long-Term Debt and Borrowings

As of December 31, 2023, Meridian Financial Corp's long-term debt totaled $7.83 billion, structured as follows:

Senior Unsecured Notes: $3.40 billion outstanding across five series with stated maturities ranging from 2025 to 2033. The weighted average coupon rate is 3.87%. The largest single maturity is a $750 million tranche due March 15, 2026, carrying a coupon of 2.95%. The Company intends to refinance this obligation through a combination of new senior note issuances and FHLB advances. The Company's senior unsecured debt is rated A3 by Moody's Investors Service, A- by S&P Global Ratings, and A by Fitch Ratings.

Subordinated Debt: $1.85 billion outstanding, qualifying as Tier 2 regulatory capital. All outstanding subordinated notes carry fixed-to-floating rate structures, converting to SOFR + 185 basis points at their respective call dates between 2028 and 2031.

Federal Home Loan Bank Advances: $2.58 billion drawn against an available credit line of $6.1 billion. These advances have a weighted average remaining maturity of 2.7 years and a weighted average cost of 4.12%. Collateral pledged to the FHLB includes $4.9 billion in first-lien residential and commercial mortgage loans.

The Company's Total Capital ratio stands at 14.7%, Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio at 11.8%, and Tier 1 Leverage ratio at 9.2%, all comfortably exceeding the well-capitalized thresholds of 10%, 6.5%, and 5%, respectively, established under the Basel III framework as implemented by U.S. banking regulators.

Annual principal maturities of long-term debt for fiscal years 2024 through 2028 are $420 million, $1.24 billion, $890 million, $640 million, and $720 million, respectively. The Company maintains adequate refinancing capacity through its established relationships with 14 primary dealer counterparties and demonstrated consistent access to capital markets over the past decade.
